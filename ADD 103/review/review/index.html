SUSPIRIA BY LUCA GUDAGNINO

The 2018 remake of Suspiria is a slow, unsettling horror film set in a Berlin dance academy run by witches. Unlike the colorful original, this version is gray and heavy, focusing on atmosphere and politics as much as scares. The dance scenes are intense and ritualistic, with one infamous sequence showing a dancer’s body grotesquely twisted. Performances, especially from Tilda Swinton and Dakota Johnson, bring depth to the strange story. It’s a disturbing, unforgiving, and harrowing film that some viewers find powerful and others find too long or confusing.

Characters and Complexity

In this version of Suspiria, the characters are given more depth than in the original. Susie transforms from a shy newcomer into a powerful figure, showing how her confidence and hidden strength grow over time. Madame Blanc, played by Tilda Swinton, isn’t just a strict teacher but a layered leader who questions her own role in the coven. Even the students, like Sara, are shown with more personality and emotion, making their struggles feel real. These choices make the story feel richer and more human, even within its supernatural horror setting.

Historical Context and the Witches

The film is set in divided Berlin during the 1970s, a time marked by political unrest and the Cold War. These real-world tensions mirror the power struggles within the dance academy, where the witches fight for control and survival. The division of the city reflects the division among the coven, showing how fear and authority shape both politics and magic.
